Intimacy Chat

  • Date: September 2020
  • Role: Full Stack Developer
  • Tools: Node.js, WebRTC, Socket.io, Bootstrap
  • Team: Livia Foldes (Art Direction and UX Design)

Intimacy Chat is an art piece and online experience that overlays the video of two participants in a real time video call. This unique interaction, combined with textual prompts mirrored to both users, asks participants to probe the tactle and emotional experience of remote communication. Livia and I were especially interested in the social dynamics of video calls, and wanted to explore how changes to the structure of the UI might change the structure of a conversation. Video calling platforms have seen little variation since the speculative designs for remote communication proposed in the early 20th century. We call on participants to assess the way this structure is taken for granted and expore new ways of building intimate relationships online.

Soft Thermocouple

  • Date: Ongoing
  • Role: Independent Researcher
  • Tools: Weaving, Embroidery, eTextiles Design, Arduino

I am currently researching a soft thermocouple for a variety of applications, especially as an alert system for extreme temperature exposure. As far as I am aware, I am the first to integrate thermocouple circuitry into a wearable system. I am currently working to optimize this system for high sensitivity readings and integrating it into a wearable computer system. I have published weekly research notes on this ongoing project on my Medium, so check there for more in-depth information on my progress.

Feedback Loop

  • Date: April 2020
  • Role: Back-End/Arduino Developer
  • Tools: Socket.io, Node.js, C++, Raspberry Pie, Arduino
  • Team: Anna E. Cook (UX Design), John Williams (Fabricator and Web Developer), Amanda Barrell (Art Direction)

Feedback Loop is an experience designed to give participants a sense of control over their lived experiences. The project takes the therapeutic practice of self-directed letters written to a future or former self and creates an accessible, live experience for people to experience globally from the comfort of their own home. The project was originally designed as a large-scale installation piece but, due to coronavirus shutdowns, the experience was adapted to an online one.

My role in this project was to recreate our project live online, through a Raspberry Pi server connected to the Arduino managing the servo motors and LED lights. Users on the website were able to see their inputs to our form change the installation live over the web through a webcam stream. I was also lucky enough to play host to the installation in my living room for the duration of the limited run.

UHF RFID Research

  • Date: December 2020
  • Role: Independent Researcher
  • Tools: Passive UHF RFID

I spent two months compiling a review of research on passive RFID in HCI applications. I was specifically interested in UHF RFID because I spent this period building a UHF RFID system for verifying the results of previous UHF RFID research around the correlation between the position and rotation of a tag in relation to the transiever and their relative signal strength indicators (RSSI).

I believe that LIDAR is extremely compelling for ubiquitous sensing solutions in consumer applications. However, LIDAR is limited to measuring objects in view of a LIDAR detector on a VR headset or mobile device. I am interested in continuing my work with radio signals, especially passive UHF RFID, to solve the problems that LIDAR cannot. I am especially interested in the low environmental cost of passive UHF RFID tags as ubiquitous sensing interface.

Brain Break

  • Date: February 2020
  • Role: Developer
  • Tools: JavaScript
  • Team: Juliet Luna (Web Design/Illustration), Liza Tolkin (Project Manager), Fiona Bell (Graphic Design), Emma Peterson (JS Developer), Corbin Peters (JS Developer)

This project was created for the 2020 T9 Hacks 24 hour hackathon at CU Boulder where Brain Break was awarded "Best Educational Software" by a team of professional educators, developers, and designers.

Brain Break is a proposed suite of products meant to improve digital hygiene in elementary age children. The app, browser extension, and website all work together to create an easy interface for parents and kids to track their browsing and reflect on whether they are making healthy decisions in their time spent online. The app and browser extension also lock children out of their digital activities after a time-limit set by the parent. Children are allowed to complete guided enrichment activities and games duing the parent-set browsing lockout period, and can earn more time by completing healthy tasks!

Hold My Heart

  • Date: October 2020
  • Role: Developer
  • Tools: Arduino
  • Team: Holiday McAllister (Fabricator)

Hold My Heart is an open-source project for building a heart that beats in time with the user’s own pulse. Until now, biometric data in art has been limited to the gallery environment. The goal of this project was to make biometric art accessible and easy for anyone with a basic understanding of physical computing and fabrication. The finished project is an uncannily realistic beating heart in an assembly that can be held in the hand, and a deceptively simple process that any hobbyist can reproduce at home. The hope is that our initial fabrication process is only the beginning of this project. See our Instructable for information on how to create the prop at home.

CU ATLAS Institute

Spring 2020

  • ATLS 2002 - Design Tech: Process
  • Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe InDesign, HTML/CSS

Process introduces students to foundational principles, practices and methods related to the process of creative design. Emphasis will be placed on the pre-production process as a creative problem-solving tools in order to produce innovative and interesting creative work. Through lectures, discussion and creative projects, students will gain a familiarity with diverse applications, methodologies, and practices related to creative technology and design. In this course, students will learn creative skills such as design thinking, empathy-based design, iteration, and design process, as well as technical skills involving UX/UI design, wireframing, web comps, moodboards, and more.
